## 1. Introduction

This Risk Disclosure explains important risks associated with using the website and interface (the “Service”) operated by Omiage.

The Service provides tools enabling users to interact with decentralized blockchain technologies, including the creation and management of on-chain profiles through the Intuition protocol deployed on the Intuition Mainnet blockchain network.

By using the Service, you acknowledge that you understand and accept the risks described below.

## 2. Blockchain Technology Risks

Blockchain technologies involve inherent risks.

These risks may include but are not limited to:
- Network failures
- Unexpected protocol behavior
- Software bugs
- Transaction delays
- Blockchain forks.

The Company does not control the blockchain network and cannot guarantee the continuous availability or reliability of blockchain infrastructure.

## 3. Smart Contract Risks

Interactions with blockchain systems may involve smart contracts, which are software programs deployed on the blockchain.

Smart contracts may contain:
- Vulnerabilities
- Coding errors
- Unforeseen behaviors.

The Company does not control or audit all smart contracts that may be interacted with through the Service.
Users assume all risks associated with interacting with smart contracts.

## 4. Irreversible Transactions

Blockchain transactions are generally irreversible.

Once a transaction has been confirmed on the blockchain:
- It cannot be reversed
- It cannot be cancelled
- It cannot be modified.

The Company cannot recover funds or reverse blockchain operations.

## 5. Public and Permanent Data

Certain features of the Service allow users to create or associate information with on-chain profiles through the Intuition protocol.

Users should understand that:
- Blockchain data may be publicly visible
- Blockchain records may be permanent
- Such information cannot easily be deleted.

Users should carefully consider what information they publish on-chain.

## 6. Wallet Security

To use certain features of the Service, users may connect a cryptocurrency wallet.

Users are solely responsible for:
- Securing their private keys
- Protecting their wallet access
- Verifying transactions before signing them.

Loss of wallet access or compromise of private keys may result in permanent loss of digital assets or data control.
The Company does not store private keys and cannot recover lost wallets.

The Company does not control these services and cannot guarantee their reliability, availability, or security.
